Cricket information

Genral Trivia

Objective: Cricket is a team sport played between two teams. The objective is to score more runs than the opposing team while batting and to dismiss all their batsmen while fielding.
Players A cricket team typically consists of 11 players, with each team taking turns to bat and field. The team that is batting is known as the "batting side," and the team that is fielding is the "bowling side."
Batting: Batsmen attempt to score runs by hitting the ball bowled by the opposition's bowlers and running between two sets of wooden stumps, called "wickets," which are placed at either end of a 22-yard-long pitch. They can also score runs by hitting the ball to the boundary rope (a four or six).
Bowling: The bowler's objective is to dismiss the batsmen by getting them out through various means, such as bowling them out, getting them caught by fielders, or having them declared out through various cricket rules. There are different formats of cricket, including:

Test Cricket: This is the longest format of the game, lasting up to five days, with each team having two innings. It is known for its strategic depth and is often considered the purest form of the game.
One day: In this format, each team is limited to 50 overs to bat and bowl.T20 is the shortest format of the game, with each team allowed 20 overs to bat and bowl. It is known for its fast-paced and entertaining nature. Governing Bodies: The International Cricket Council (ICC) is the global governing body for cricket. It oversees international matches and tournaments, including the Cricket World Cup and various Test and T20 leagues.
Famous Tournaments: Some of the most well-known cricket tournaments include the ICC Cricket World Cup, the ICC T20 World Cup, The Ashes (a Test series between England and Australia), the Indian Premier League (IPL), and various domestic leagues in different countries.
Cricketing Legends: Over the years, cricket has seen many legendary players, such as Sachin Tendulkar, Sir Don Bradman, Sir Vivian Richards, Sir Garfield Sobers, and many more, who have left an indelible mark on the sport.
Spirit: Cricket places a strong emphasis on sportsmanship and fair play. It is often referred to as the "gentleman's game."
Popularity: Cricket is one of the most popular sports in the world, particularly in countries like India, where it's almost a religion, and in England and Australia, where it has a rich history.

Ranking rules

Team Rankings: These rankings include men's and women's teams in various formats of the game, including Test cricket, One Day Internationals (ODIs), and Twenty20 Internationals (T20Is). Teams are ranked based on their performance in recent matches, with points awarded for wins and taken away for losses. The rankings are used to determine the top-ranked teams in each format.
Player Rankings: These rankings cover individual players' performances in all three formats of the game. Players are ranked based on their batting, bowling, and all-rounder abilities. Batsmen and bowlers are ranked separately, and there are separate rankings for men and women.
Team and Player Rankings for Test, ODI, and T20I Formats: Each format has its own set of rankings. These rankings help fans and cricket enthusiasts gauge the performance and standing of teams and players in specific formats.
Team Rankings for Test Championship The ICC Test Championship rankings are part of the World Test Championship (WTC) system, which was introduced to add context and competition to Test cricket. The rankings in this format determine which teams are leading in the WTC.
Player Rankings for Emerging Cricketers: This ranking system highlights emerging talents and young players who have shown promise in international cricket.

Records

Team Records: Highest Team Score in Test Cricket: The record for the highest team score in a Test match is held by Sri Lanka, which scored 952/6 against India in 1997.
Lowest Team Score in Test Cricket: New Zealand bowled out Bangladesh for just 26 runs in 1955, which remains the lowest team score in Test cricket.
Highest Team Score in One Day Internationals (ODIs): England holds the record for the highest team score in ODIs, with 481/6 against Australia in 2018.
Lowest Team Score in ODIs: Zimbabwe was bowled out for 35 runs by Sri Lanka in 2004, setting the record for the lowest team score in ODIs.
Highest Team Score in T20 Internationals: Afghanistan scored 278/3 against Ireland in 2019, which is the highest team score in T20Is.
Most Consecutive Wins in Test Cricket: Australia holds the record for the most consecutive Test match wins, with 16 wins between 1999 andv 2001.
Individual Records:
Most Runs in Test Cricket: Sachin Tendulkar of India holds the record for the most runs in Test cricket, with 15,921 runs.
Most Wickets in Test Cricket: Muttiah Muralitharan of Sri Lanka is the highest wicket-taker in Test cricket, with 800 wickets.
Most Runs in ODI Cricket: Sachin Tendulkar also holds the record for the most runs in ODI cricket, with 18,426 runs.
Most Wickets in ODI Cricket: Muttiah Muralitharan is the highest wicket-taker in ODI cricket as well, with 534 wickets.
Fastest Century in ODIs: AB de Villiers of South Africa holds the record for the fastest century in ODIs, reaching his hundred in just 31 balls against the West Indies in 2015.
Most Sixes in T20 Internationals: Rohit Sharma of India holds the record for the most sixes in T20 Internationals.
Highest Individual Score in Test Cricket: Brian Lara of the West Indies scored 400 not out against England in 2004, which is the highest individual score in Test cricket.
Most Sixes in a Single ODI Innings: Eoin Morgan of England hit 17 sixes in a single ODI innings against Afghanistan in 2019, setting a record for the most sixes in an ODI.
